What Makes Wind and Ocean Currents Move?

1. The Sun Heats Earth Unevenly

The equator gets blasted with direct sunlight but the poles barely get any because of the angle. This difference in heating is basically what starts everything. Hot air at the equator rises up and cool air at the poles sinks down.

2. Earth's Rotation

Since Earth is spinning, things don't move in straight lines. Moving air and water get pushed to the right in the Northern Hemisphere and to the left in the Southern Hemisphere.

3. How Air Moves

Hot air goes up, cold air goes down.

4. Ocean Currents Follow the Wind

When wind blows across the ocean, it pushes the water along. Warm water moves from the equator to the poles and cold water moves back.

City Climate Analysis

Similarities: They're all on the coast so they all deal with winds and ocean currents. Most are around the same distance from the equator.

Differences: East coast cities like Miami and Tokyo have warm ocean currents, so they're hot and humid. West coast cities like Lima and Seattle have cold currents, so they're cooler and drier. It makes a huge difference.

Greater Impact: Ocean currents matter more than winds for these cities. Water stays hot or cold way longer than air, so whatever temperature the ocean is really affects the land nearby. That's why Lima is basically a desert even though it's right on the coast.

Questions and Answers

Q1: Why do prevailing winds and ocean surface currents move in similar patterns?

Wind pushes the ocean water and both get curved by Earth's spinning.

Q2: How would these patterns change if there were no landmasses?

Currents would go all the way around the planet in circles and winds would be steadier.

Q3: What would these patterns look like if Earth did not rotate?

Everything would move in straight lines north and south instead of curving.

Q4: Which wind system has the strongest effect on local climate? How does it affect our climate?

The westerlies blow weather across the country from west to east and give us four seasons.

Q5: Do any ocean currents affect our climate? If so, how?

The Gulf Stream makes winters milder and adds moisture to the air along the coast.
